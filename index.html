About Miss Arnold

Hi, I’m Claire.

I’m a certified middle school social studies teacher with over five years of tutoring experience supporting students in grades 1 through 8. Through my work with students and families, I’ve seen how quickly confidence can shift when a child begins to understand the material and feels supported in the process.

Miss Arnold’s Tutoring Table was created to provide more than just tutoring. My goal is to help students strengthen the academic skills they need while also building the confidence and independence that helps them succeed in school long term.

Families often come to me when school has started to feel frustrating, overwhelming, or discouraging for their child. Together, we work step by step to rebuild confidence, strengthen foundational skills, and create steady academic growth.

The Build & Believe Approach

A Clear Plan + Consistent Support = Real Confidence

Confidence isn’t built through pressure — it’s built through structure, small wins, and consistency. The Build & Believe Approach blends targeted skill-building with confidence-centered support so students learn how to approach school with trust in themselves.

What this looks like week to week

Sessions are intentional and focused. We work on the right skills at the right pace, track progress, and build habits that help students stick with challenges instead of shutting down.

Parents are kept in the loop with clear updates so you know what’s improving and what we’re working on next.

What Families Often Notice Within the First Month

As students begin consistent mentorship, families often start to notice meaningful changes both academically and emotionally.

  • ✓Homework frustration begins to decrease
    Less shutdown, less pushback, more willingness to try.
  • ✓Confidence begins to grow
    Students start believing they can figure things out.
  • ✓Skills become more consistent
    Foundations strengthen and accuracy improves over time.
  • ✓More independent attempts
    Students begin trying problems before asking for help.
  • ✓Parents feel more supported
    Clear updates and next steps reduce uncertainty at home.

These changes develop gradually through consistent practice, encouragement, and structured skill-building.
